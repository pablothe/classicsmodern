Raskolnikov entered almost at the same moment as they returned from the cemetery.
Katrina Ivanovna was terribly delighted with him, firstly, because he was the only “educated guest”
among all the guests, and “as is known, he was preparing to take up a professorship at the university in
two years,”

In the second place, because he immediately and respectfully apologized to her for not being able to attend the funeral, despite all his desire; she then pounced on him, seated him at the table to her right (Amalia Ivanovna sat to her left) and, despite the constant bustle and efforts to ensure that the food was served properly and everyone received their share, despite the agonizing cough that intermittently interrupted and choked her, and seemed to have taken root particularly in these last two days, she continuously addressed Raskolnikov in a low whisper, rushing to pour out all the accumulated feelings and her justified indignation regarding the failed memorial service; moreover, the indignation was often replaced by a frequently joyful, unrestrained laughter at the assembled guests, but primarily at the hostess herself.
