If he were to prove that Sofya Semonovna is a thief, firstly, he would prove to my sister and mother that he was almost right in his suspicions; that he justly angered himself because I put my sister and Sofya Semenovna on the same board; that, attacking me, he defended, therefore, and protected the honor of my sister, and of his fiancée. In one word, through all this he could even again quarrel with me with my relatives and, of course, hoped to again enter their grace. I don't even say that he was avenging himself personally on me, because he has reason to suppose that the honor and happiness of Sofya Semenovna are very dear to me. That is the whole of his calculation! That is how I understand this matter! And there is no other reason for it!

Thus or almost thus concluded Raskolnikov his speech, frequently interrupted by exclamations of the audience, who were listening very attentively. But despite all the interruptions, he spoke sharply, calmly, accurately, clearly, firmly. His sharp voice, his convinced tone and strict face produced an extraordinary effect on everyone.

— So, so, this is so! — Lebednikov confirmed in delight. — It must be so, because he precisely asked me as soon as Sofya Semenovna entered our room, “Are you here? Have I seen you among the guests of Ekaterina Ivanovna?” He called me for this to the window and there quietly asked. Therefore, he certainly needed you to be here. This is so, this is all so!

Luzhin remained silent and sneered contemptuously. However, he was very pale.

It seemed that he was considering how to get out of it. Perhaps he would gladly drop everything and leave, but at this very moment it was almost impossible; it meant directly admitting the truth of the accusations leveled against him and that he had indeed slandered Sofya Semenovna.
