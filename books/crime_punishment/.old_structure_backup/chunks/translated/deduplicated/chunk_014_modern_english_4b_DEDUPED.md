She was again lost, but this last loss did not last long.
Her pale-yellow, shriveled face flung itself backward, her mouth opened, her legs stretched convulsively. She took a deep, deep breath and died.

Sonia fell on her corpse, embraced it with her hands and remained frozen, leaning her head to the deceased’s shriveled chest. Poleyka knelt at her mother’s feet and kissed them, crying hysterically. Kolya and Lena, not yet understanding what had happened, but foreseeing something very frightening, grabbed each other by the shoulders with both hands and, staring at each other with their eyes, suddenly opened their mouths together and began to scream. Both were still in costumes: one in a helmet, the other in a hat with a swan feather.

And how did this “commendation sheet” suddenly find itself on the bed, beside that of Ekaterina Ivanovna? It lay right there, by the pillow; Raskolnikov had seen it.

He moved to the window. Lebednikov rushed up to him.

— She is dead! — Lebednikov said.

— Rodion Romanovich, I have two necessary words to convey to you, — approached Svidryгайlov. Lebednikov immediately yielded his place and shuffled delicately. Svidryгайlov led the surprised Raskolnikov even further into the corner.
